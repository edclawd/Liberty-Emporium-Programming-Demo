About

Not an agency.
A real person who builds real things.

I’m Ronald J. Alexander Jr. — a self-taught developer, computer technician, and small business owner based in Liberty, North Carolina. I’ve been working with computers since before most people knew what the internet was.

For 13 years I ran Liberty PC Repair, providing hardware upgrades, network setup, technical support, and Windows server environments for customers all over Randolph County — everything from home PCs to full classroom deployments. I still run this business in my spare time.

I also spent 3 years in Research & Development at Thermaco in Asheboro, managing new product development, production, and inventory. And 12 years at EIC (Engineered Ideas and Concepts) operating CNC Lathe machines, CNC Brake machines, Drill Press, Power Shear, and Forklift — so I understand manufacturing and physical inventory from the ground up.

When I opened Liberty Emporium & Thrift, I needed software to track inventory, manage photos, and get products listed online fast. Everything I found was too expensive or too complicated for a small store. So I built my own.

The result is a full-stack web app with AI-powered photo analysis, ad generation, price tag printing, Facebook Marketplace listing generation, and multi-user access — running live at my store at 125 W. Swannanoa Ave., Liberty, NC. Now I’m offering to set it up for other small businesses. You get the same system I use every day, customized with your brand, at a price that makes sense for a small shop.
